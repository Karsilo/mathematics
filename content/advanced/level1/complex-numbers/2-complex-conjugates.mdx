---
title: 2. Complex Conjugates
description: -----
---

Two complex numbers that are mirror images across the real axis.

Let **$\; \; \;  z = a + b i$** 

then **$\; \overline{z} = a - b i \; $** is called the complex conjugate of $z$.

&nbsp;

### Example

<p style={{ marginBottom: '0.5em' }}></p>

Write $\dfrac{5 + 4i}{2 - 3i}$ in the form $a + b i$.


$$
\begin{aligned}
\dfrac{5 + 4i}{2 - 3i} 
&= \dfrac{(5 + 4i)(2 + 3i)}{(2 - 3i)(2 + 3i)} \\[3ex]
&= \dfrac{10 + 15i + 8i + 12i^2}{4 + 6i - 6i - 9i^2} \\[3ex]
&= \dfrac{-2 + 23i}{13} \\[3ex]
&= -\dfrac{2}{13} + \dfrac{23}{13} i
\end{aligned}
$$
