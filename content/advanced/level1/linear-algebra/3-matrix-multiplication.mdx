## Definition

#### Associativity

- $(a + b) + c = a + (b + c)$
- $(a \times b) \times c = a \times (b \times c)$

#### Distributivity
- $a(b + c) = ab + ac$

#### Commutativity 
- $ab = ba$
- This result is possible but not guaranteed

#### Properties of Matrix Multiplication
- Associative & Distributive
- Not Cummutative


## Derivations

If we have two matrices with dimensions $r_1 \times c_1$ and $r_2 \times c_2$

these two matrices can only be multiplied if $c_1=r_2$

the resultant matrix will have dimensions $r_1 \times c_2$

say we have two matrices $A$ and $B$ and we want to find $AB$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
e & f \\
g & h
\end{bmatrix}
= 
\begin{bmatrix}
ae +bg & af + bh \\
ce + dg & cf + dh
\end{bmatrix}
$$

**What have we just done?**

**Step 1:** Take each item in the first row of $A$ and multiply it by its respective item in the first column of $B$

**Step 2:** Add these together and this gives us the value at position $(1, 1)$

**Step 3:** Take each item in the first row of $A$ and multiply it by its respective item in the second column of $B$

**Step 4:** Add these together and this gives us the value at position $(1, 2)$

**Step 5:** Repeat for the second row of $A$

### Example

Calculate the result of $AB$

$$
A = 
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 6 & 1 \\
0 & 7 & -3
\end{bmatrix}
, \quad \quad B =
\begin{bmatrix}
8 & -4 \\
1 & 3 \\
0 & -2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

#### Answer

$$
AB = 
\begin{bmatrix}
(3 \times 8) + (6 \times 1) + (1 \times 0) & (3 \times -4) + (6 \times 3) + (1 \times -2) \\
(0 \times 8) + (7 \times 1) + (-3 \times 0) & (0 \times -4) + (7 \times 3) + (-3 \times -2)
\end{bmatrix}
$$

this simplifies to

$$
AB = 
\begin{bmatrix}
30 & 4 \\
7 & 27
\end{bmatrix}
$$
