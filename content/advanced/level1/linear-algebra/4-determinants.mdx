## Definitions

#### Determinant
- A scalar value linked to a square matrix **(area scale factor)**
- Only square matrices have determinants

#### Singular Matrix
- $\det(M) = 0 $
- The determinant is zero

&nbsp;

## Derivations

#### 2x2

$$
M = 
\begin{bmatrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{bmatrix}
\quad \text{then} \quad \det(M) = 
\begin{vmatrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{vmatrix}
= 
ad - bc
$$

#### 3x3

$$
M = 
\begin{bmatrix}
a & b & c \\
d & e & f \\
g & h & i
\end{bmatrix}

$$

then

$$
\det(M) = 
\begin{vmatrix}
a & b & c \\
d & e & f \\
g & h & i
\end{vmatrix}
= 
a
\begin{vmatrix}
e & f \\
h & i
\end{vmatrix}
+ b
\begin{vmatrix}
f & d \\
i & g
\end{vmatrix}
+ c
\begin{vmatrix}
d & e \\
g & h
\end{vmatrix}
$$

simplifies to

$$
a(ei - fh) + b(fg - di) + c(dh - eg)
$$

or 

$$
aei + bfg + cdh - afh - bdi - ceg
$$

notice that this result is the same as multiplying the diagonals

we sum the diagonals that go left to right

then subtract the diagonals that go right to left
