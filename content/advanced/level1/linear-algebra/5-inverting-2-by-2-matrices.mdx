## Definition

The inverse of $M$ is denoted by $M^{-1}$

$$
MM^{-1} = M^{-1}M = I
$$

&nbsp;

## Derivations

Let $M = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$ then

$$
M^{-1} = \dfrac{1}{\det(M)} 
\begin{bmatrix}
d & -b \\
-c & a
\end{bmatrix}
$$

notice that since we divide by the determinant, a singular matrix cannot be inverted because its determinant is zero

&nbsp;

### Example

Find the inverse of $M = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 5 \\ 1 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$

#### Answer

$$
\det(M) = (3 \times 6) - (5 \times 1) = 12
$$

then 

$$
M^{-1} = \dfrac{1}{12}
\begin{bmatrix}
6 & -5 \\
-1 & 3
\end{bmatrix}
$$
