## Definitions

A matrix is an ordered array of numbers

#### Regular Matrix

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
7 & 3 & 9 \\
\dfrac{1}{2} & 4 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

this is a $2\times3$ matrix (2 rows & 3 columns)

&nbsp;

#### Square Matrix

The number of rows and number of columns are equal

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
a & b \\
c & d
\end{bmatrix}
$$

&nbsp;

#### Zero Matrix

All values in the matrix are zero

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 \\
0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

&nbsp;

#### Identity Matrix

Multiplying one matrix ($M1$) by the identity matrix ($I$) gives you ($M1$)

$$
I =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
0 & 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

or 

$$
I =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

all identity matrices are square

&nbsp;

### Scalar Operations

#### Scalar Addition

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 \\
-2 & 4 \\
\end{bmatrix}
+
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 1 \\
8 & 5 \\
\end{bmatrix}
= 
\begin{bmatrix}
4 & 1 \\
6 & 9 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

&nbsp;

#### Scalar Multiplication

$$
4
\begin{bmatrix}
3 & 1 \\
8 & 5 \\
\end{bmatrix}
= 
\begin{bmatrix}
12 & 4 \\
32 & 20 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
