## Definition

Used to evaluate infinite sums

Length squared of a periodic function $f(x)$

$$
||f(x)||^2 = (f(x), f(x)) = \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (f(x))^2 \; dx
$$

finding the inner product

$$
||f(x)||^2 = (\; 1 \cdot a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx), \; 1 \cdot a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx) \;)
$$

simplify

$$
||f(x)||^2 = a_0^2 (1, 1) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n^2 (\cos(nx), \cos(nx)) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n^2 (\sin(nx), \sin(nx))
$$

this comes out to

$$
||f(x)||^2 = 2\pi (a_0)^2 + \pi \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n)^2 + (b_n)^2
$$
