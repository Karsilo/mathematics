## Definition

The Inner Product between two periodic functions $f(x), g(x)$ of period $2\pi$

$$
(f(x), g(x)) = \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x)g(x) \; dx
$$

### Orthogonal functions

When $(f(x), g(x)) = 0$ then the functions are said to be orthogonal

The blue 'basis' periodic functions in the set $\; \{\; 1, \; \cos(nx), \; \sin(nx) \; \} \;$ are all mutually orthogonal to one another

## Cases

$$
(1, 1) = 2\pi
$$

&nbsp;

$$
(\sin(mx), \sin(nx)) = \begin{cases}
0 & \text{for} \quad m \neq n \\
\pi & \text{for} \quad m = n
\end{cases}
$$

&nbsp;

$$
(\cos(mx), \cos(nx)) = \begin{cases}
0 & \text{for} \quad m \neq n \\
\pi & \text{for} \quad m = n
\end{cases}
$$
