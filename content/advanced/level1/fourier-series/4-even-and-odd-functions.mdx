## Definition

$$
f(x) = \begin{cases}
\text{even} & \text{if } \quad f(x) = f(-x) \\
\text{odd} & \text{if } \quad f(x) = -f(-x)
\end{cases}
$$

### Cases

$$
\begin{align*}
\sin(nx) &= -\sin(-nx) \quad \quad \text{ODD} \\

\cos(nx) &= \cos(-nx) \quad \quad \text{EVEN} \\

1(x) &= 1(-x) = 1 \quad \quad \text{EVEN} \\

x &= -(-x) \quad \quad \text{ODD} \\

x\cos(nx) &= -(-x)\cos(-nx) \quad \text{ODD} \\

x\sin(nx) &= -x\sin(-nx) \quad \text{EVEN} \\
\end{align*}
$$

&nbsp;

## Even Functions

If $f(x)$ is EVEN

$$
\begin{align*}
a_0 &= \dfrac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \; dx &= \dfrac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (EVEN) \; dx &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) \; dx \\
\; \\
a_n &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x)\cos(nx) \; dx &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (EVEN) \; dx &= \dfrac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x)\cos(nx) \; dx \\
\; \\
b_n &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x)\sin(nx) \; dx &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (ODD) \; dx &= 0
\end{align*}
$$

&nbsp;

## Odd Functions

If $f(x)$ is ODD

$$
\begin{align*}
a_0 &= \dfrac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \; dx = \dfrac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (ODD) \; dx = 0 \\
\; \\
a_n &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x)\cos(nx) \; dx = \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (ODD) \; dx = 0 \\
\; \\
b_n &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x)\sin(nx) \; dx = \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} (EVEN) \; dx = \dfrac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x)\sin(nx) \; dx
\end{align*}
$$
