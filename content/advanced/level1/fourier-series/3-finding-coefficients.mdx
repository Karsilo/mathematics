## Derivation

$$
f(x) = 1\cdot a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx)
$$

To find any coefficient $a_n$

$$
(f(x), \cos(nx)) = (\; 1 \cdot a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx), \; \cos(nx))
$$

this gives

$$
a_0(1, \cos(nx)) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n(\cos(nx), \cos(nx)) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n(\sin(nx), \cos(nx))
$$

we can simplify this 

$$
a_0 \cdot 0 + a_n \pi + b_n \cdot 0 = a_n \pi
$$

therefore

$$
a_n = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) \; dx
$$


## Key Equations

For any coefficient

$$
a_0 = \dfrac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \; dx
$$
&nbsp;
$$
a_n = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) \; dx
$$
&nbsp;
$$
b_n = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \sin(nx) \; dx
$$
