## Definition

For function with periodicity $2L$ such that $f(x + 2L) = f(x)$ we define a function $g(x) = f(\dfrac{Lx}{\pi})$, this function $g(x)$ has a period of $2\pi$

$$
g(x + 2\pi) = f(\dfrac{L(x + 2\pi)}{\pi}) = f(\dfrac{Lx}{\pi} + 2L) = f(\dfrac{Lx}{\pi}) = g(x)
$$

the function $f(\dfrac{Lx}{\pi})$ has a period of $2\pi$ so

$$
f(\dfrac{Lx}{\pi}) = g(x) = a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx)
$$

we define $z = \dfrac{Lx}{\pi}$ so $x = \dfrac{z\pi}{L}$ and thus

$$
f(z) = a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(\dfrac{\pi n z}{L}) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(\dfrac{\pi n z}{L})
$$

## Solving for Coefficients

$$
\begin{align*}
a_n &= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} g(x)\cos(nx) \; dx \\
\; \\
&= \dfrac{1}{\pi} \int_{-L}^{L} f(z)\cos(nx) \dfrac{\pi}{L} \; dz \\
\; \\
&= \dfrac{1}{L} \int_{-L}^{L} f(z)\cos(nx) \; dz
\end{align*}
$$
