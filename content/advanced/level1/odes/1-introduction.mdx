---
title: 1. Introduction
description: An ordinary differential equation (ODE) is an equation that relates a function of one variable to its derivatives with respect to that variable.
---


### Autonomous ODE

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx} = f(y)
$$

when the right hand side only depends on $y$

#### Example

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx} - 2y = 0
$$

&nbsp;

### Non-Autonomous ODE

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx} = f(x, y)
$$

when the right hand side depends on $x$ and $y$

#### Example

$$
\dfrac{dy}{dx} - 2xy = 3x
$$


&nbsp;

### Simple first order ODE

<p style={{ marginBottom: '0.5em' }}></p>

$$
\quad \dfrac{dy}{dx} + f(x)y = g(x)
$$

&nbsp;

### Bernoulli ODE

<p style={{ marginBottom: '0.5em' }}></p>

$$
\quad \dfrac{dy}{dx} + f(x)y = g(x)y^n \quad \text{where} \quad n \neq 0, 1 
$$

&nbsp;

### 2nd Order Homogenous ODE

<p style={{ marginBottom: '0.5em' }}></p>


$$
\quad a\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2} + b\dfrac{dy}{dx} + cy = 0
$$

&nbsp;

### 2nd Order Inhomogenous ODE

<p style={{ marginBottom: '0.5em' }}></p>


$$
\quad a\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2} + b\dfrac{dy}{dx} + cy = f(x)
$$
