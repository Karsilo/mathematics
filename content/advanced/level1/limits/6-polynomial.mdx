How to take the limit with one polynomial over another when the limit looks like it will be $\dfrac{0}{0}$

### Example

$$
\lim_{0 \to 3} \dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x^3 - 27}
$$

#### Answer

Factorise 

$$
x^2 - 9 = (x + 3)(x - 3)
$$

$$
x^3 - 27 = (x - 3)(x^2 + 3x + 9)
$$

since $x \neq 3$

$$
\dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x^3 - 27} = \dfrac{x - 3}{x^2 + 3x + 9}
$$

therefore

$$
\lim_{x \to 3} \dfrac{x - 3}{x^2 + 3x + 9} = \dfrac{3 + 3}{9 + 9 + 9} = \boxed{\dfrac{2}{9}}
$$
