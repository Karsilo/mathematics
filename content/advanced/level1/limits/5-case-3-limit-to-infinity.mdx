### Case 3

Find the $N$ & $\epsilon$ that satisfies 

$$
\lim_{x \to \pm\infty}\dfrac{1}{x} = 0
$$

#### Answer

For limits at infinity, we use the $\epsilon-N$ definition

For any $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $N > 0$ such that

$$
|x| > N \quad \to \quad |\dfrac{1}{x} - 0| < \epsilon
$$

**Step 1:** Write the inequaltiy in terms of x

$$
|\dfrac{1}{x}| < \epsilon
$$

**Step 2:** Solve for $|x|$

$$
|x| > \dfrac{1}{\epsilon}
$$

**Step 3:** Choose $N$

$$
N = \dfrac{1}{\epsilon}
$$
