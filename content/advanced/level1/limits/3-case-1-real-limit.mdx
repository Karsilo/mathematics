### Case 1

Find the $\delta$ & $\epsilon$ that satisfies 

$$
\lim_{x \to 1} 5x + 2 = 7
$$

#### Answer

**Step 1:** Express $|f(x) - L| < \epsilon$ in terms of $x$
$$
|f(x) - L| \; = \; |(5x + 2) - 7| \; = \; |5(x - 1)|
$$

**Step 2:** Relate it to $\epsilon$

$$
|f(x) - L| < \epsilon \quad \to \quad |5(x - 1)| < \epsilon
$$

**Step 3:** Solve for $\epsilon$

$$
|x - 1| < \frac{\epsilon}{5}
$$

**Step 4:** Choose $\delta$

$$
\delta = \frac{\epsilon}{5}
$$

**Note:** saying $|x - 1| < \dfrac{\epsilon}{n} \quad$ where $\quad n \in \mathbb{R}, \; n \geq 3 \quad$ so $\quad \delta > \dfrac{\epsilon}{n} \quad$ is also true
